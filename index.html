Priority 1 · every Monday

ELT weekly reporting

The backbone of everything. A Monday-to-Thursday workflow that turns raw data into a strategy-ready story for ELT — covering the full funnel from traffic to trials to ARR, by source, region, and onboarding signal.

Data pulled Monday
  • Traffic by source: paid, organic, direct
  • Top 12 priority countries + other bucket
  • MQL volume, demos, pricing requests
  • Trial count by region and source
  • Switcher data: who they left, conversion rate
  • Onboarding answers: size, contacts, experience
The funnel we measure
  • Traffic → MQL (scored behavioral signals)
  • MQL → trial (primary company KPI)
  • Trial → demo or pricing request
  • Trial → conversion (ARR — priority 1)
  • Who is buying big and why
Weekly workflow
  • Monday 9–11am: pull and validate (Alicia + Nicholas)
  • Tuesday: deep dive on ups/downs, Airtable context
  • Wednesday: build the story, organize the narrative
  • Thursday: polish and present to ELT
Why it matters
Aligned RevOps reporting drives 36% more revenue growth. Clean Monday data is the foundation — scoring, Airtable context, and campaign attribution all depend on it. When a country moves, we need the story, not just the number.

Eight bold recommendations — outcomes first, data required
Notes save automatically in this browser
01
Website prompt box — capture leads before they decide
Add an interactive prompt to the AC website: "What do you want to automate?" Get their website URL. Ask 2 qualifying questions (business type, contact count). Then ask for email — after they're invested. Even if they don't start a trial, we have them. Measure incomplete trial starts as a campaign funnel stage. Build nurture sequences around that. This is a category-defining lead capture improvement and a massive missed opportunity right now.
Data needed
Current trial abandonment rate · email capture rate pre-trial · incomplete trial volume · nurture campaign performance on partial signups
02
Dynamic testing framework — know before you scale
Before scaling any channel, message, or campaign, establish a structured A/B testing program. Headlines, subject lines, CTAs, onboarding steps, ad creatives — everything should have a winning version before it gets budget. The companies winning right now aren't guessing. They're testing systematically and moving fast on what works.
Data needed
Baseline conversion rates by channel · test infrastructure audit · statistical significance thresholds · 8-week testing calendar
03
Social media strategy — what does this actually look like?
YouTube tutorials for power users who want to see AC in action before they buy. Instagram for mid-market owners who decide with their eyes. TikTok for SMB decision-makers under 40 who learn in 60 seconds. AC is a product people love once they understand it — social closes the discovery gap. 73% of B2B marketers call social media effective for their business. We need a plan, not just a presence.
Data needed
Current social traffic contribution (ELT pull) · competitor social audit · platform engagement benchmarks · content production resource plan
04
Country-specific seasonal campaigns — meet them in their moment
Fashion Week in Milan and New York. Carnival in Brazil. Golden Week in Japan. Diwali business season in India. These are the moments when mid-sophisticated businesses are actively investing in tools and growth. We should be there with localized messaging and cultural intelligence that says we see them. Localized campaigns: +29% open rates, +41% click rates.
Data needed
Top-12 country performance by month · industry vertical by country · cultural calendar research · localization capability audit
05
Industry vertical targeting — same tool, different stories
Healthcare, fintech, and retail are the fastest-growing SaaS verticals. A healthcare practice uses AC completely differently than a fashion retailer. Dynamic messaging by vertical — lifecycle emails, ad copy, landing pages — is the highest-leverage, lowest-cost multiplier available to us right now. We're not building new product. We're telling the right story to the right buyer at the right moment.
Data needed
Trial data by industry from intake form · conversion rate by vertical · vertical-specific messaging baseline · SaaS penetration benchmarks by sector
06
Behavioral activation scoring — conversion without headcount
Trial users who invite a teammate or complete a key onboarding action convert at dramatically higher rates. Defining 3 activation milestones and building automated nudges around them turns the existing trial base into a conversion engine that runs on its own. Behavior-triggered sequences activate 3.1x more users to "aha moment" milestones vs. static email drip.
Data needed
Product usage mapped to trial-to-paid conversion · onboarding completion rates by step · 30/60/90-day retention by activation action
07
First-party data moat — we already have what others pay for
Our intake form answers — company size, contact count, switching from, experience level — are a competitive intelligence asset most SaaS companies spend years collecting. With third-party cookies gone, this signal is our edge. Using it to personalize trial onboarding by segment doesn't require new technology. It requires using what we already have.
Data needed
Intake form responses mapped to conversion · personalized vs. generic onboarding A/B test design · lifecycle team coordination plan
08
Switcher conversion playbook — they've already decided to leave
People leaving Mailchimp and HubSpot have already made the hard decision. They just haven't chosen where yet. A dedicated switcher track — landing page, migration support, onboarding sequence built around their specific pain points — captures the highest-intent prospects in the entire funnel. Build once for Mailchimp. Replicate for every competitor. The template is already in flight.
Data needed
Current switcher conversion rate (ELT pull) · days-to-conversion for switchers vs. organic · competitor migration pain point research · lifecycle team capacity
